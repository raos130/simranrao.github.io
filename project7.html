Director's Editions
EDITORIAL

A series of publications highlighting some of the best directors in Hollywood by encapsulating their thematic styles and the tones that recur throughout their filmographies.



ALFRED HITCHCOCK


This publication was inspired by the posters from some of Hitchcock's most iconic films; Vertigo and Just Dial M for Murder, as well as his themes of danger and suspense, which inspired the typographical choices and the color palette. The narrow columns of text are reminiscent of his simple yet dynamic style of story-telling, which is making something multi-faceted with a few, basic elements, as seen in Rear Window.



DENIS VILLENEUVE


Villeneuve is known for his dark, psychological thrillers complete with a mind-blowing plot twist. To encapsulate the tone of his films and the strong visual aesthetics in them, I chose to use a stark color palette. The flipped text plays with his themes of chaos and twist endings as well as the introspective nature of his films, as seen in Enemy and Prisoners.



CHRISTOPHER NOLAN


This publication was inspired by Nolan's signature style of film-making. To represent his themes of identity and doubles, as seen in Inception and The Prestige, I used multiple fonts and contrasted each alternate page to create a dynamic sense of repetition. Another staple element in his films, is his non-linear way of story-telling. The offset emphasized text is a break from this publications' set guide of narrating content.
